India has exceptional full‑stack talent—but **how you hire** matters as much as the tech stack. Whether you need React, Node, or a full **MERN** build, a structured process saves money and avoids late‑project surprises.

## Start with outcomes, not buzzwords

Before you post a job, write one paragraph: **what “done” looks like** in two months. Examples: “customers can sign up and pay,” “admin can import CSVs,” “site ranks for three target keywords.” Generic “we need a full‑stack dev” attracts mismatched applicants.

## Vet technical depth quickly

Ask for **one past project** closest to yours and dig into:

- How they handled **auth**, **payments**, or **background jobs** (if relevant).
- How they’d **deploy** and monitor—Vercel, Docker, logs, alerts.
- **Tradeoffs** they made on deadlines vs. quality.

A strong freelancer explains decisions; a weak one only lists tools.

## Contracts and scope

Agree in writing on:

- **Milestones** tied to payments, not open‑ended hourly buckets (unless you truly want advisory hours).
- **Ownership** of code and assets after final payment.
- **Communication rhythm**—standups, async updates, and a single source of truth (Linear, Notion, etc.).

If you are comparing proposals, the cheapest quote is rarely the lowest **total cost** once rework is counted.

## Time zones and collaboration

India ↔ US/EU overlap is limited, but it works when you batch feedback, use **written specs**, and record Looms for async review. Ask candidates how they’ve **shipped with overseas clients** before—patterns matter more than perfect overlap.

## Red flags to trust

- No public **portfolio**, GitHub, or case studies you can verify.
- Unwilling to use **version control** or share a staging environment.
- Promises “SEO #1 in a week” or “unlimited revisions” with no boundaries.

## When a specialist beats a generalist

If you are committed to **Next.js** and care about **Core Web Vitals** and SEO, hiring someone who ships **App Router** projects regularly often beats a resume that lists twenty frameworks.
